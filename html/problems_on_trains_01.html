1] A train running at the speed of 60 km/hr crosses a pole in 9 seconds. What is the length of the train?

A] 120 meters

B] 120 meters

C] 120 meters

D] 120 meters

Answer: Option C
Solution:

$$ \eqalign{ & {\text{Speed of the train relative to man}}\cr & = {\frac{{125}}{{10}}} {\text{ m/sec}} \cr & = {\frac{{25}}{2}} {\text{ m/sec}} \cr & = {\frac{{25}}{2} \times \frac{{18}}{5}} {\text{ km/hr}} \cr & = 45\,{\text{km/hr}} \cr & {\text{Let}}\,{\text{the}}\,{\text{speed}}\,{\text{of}}\,{\text{the}}\,{\text{train}}\,{\text{be}}\,x\,{\text{km/hr}}. \cr & \text{Then, relative speed} = \left( {x - 5} \right)\,{\text{km/hr}} \cr & \therefore x - 5 = 45 \cr & \Rightarrow x = 50\,{\text{km/hr}} \cr} $$ $$
