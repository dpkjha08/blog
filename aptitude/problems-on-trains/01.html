1] A 160 meters long train crosses a 160 meters long platform in 16 seconds. Find the speed of the train.
Answer: Option B
Solution:
$$ \eqalign{ &\text{Distance covered in passing the platform} \cr &= (160 + 160)m \cr &= 320m \cr &\therefore \text{Speed of train} \cr &= \frac{320}{16}m/sec = 20 m/sec \cr &= \left( 20 \times \frac{18}{5} \right)km/hr \cr &= 72 km/hr } $$

2] A person standing on a railway platform noticed that a train took 21 seconds to completely pass through the platform which was 84 m long and it took 9 seconds in passing him. Find the speed of the train in kmph.
Answer: Option D
Solution:
$$ \eqalign{ & \text{Let the length of the train be x meters} \cr & \text{Then, the train covers x meters in 9 sec} \cr & \text{and (x + 84) meters in 21 seconds.} \cr & \text{So, length of the train = 63m} \cr & \text{Speed of the train}\cr & = \left(\frac{63}{9} \right)m/sec = 7m/sec \cr & = \left( 7 \times \frac{18}{5}\right)km/hr \cr & = \left( \frac{126}{5}\right)km/hr \cr & = 25.2 km/hr. } $$

3] A train is travelling with constant speed crosses a 90 m long platform in 12 seconds and a 120 m long platform in 15 seconds. Find the length of the train and its speed.
Answer: Option C
Solution:
$$ \eqalign{ & \text{Let the length of the train be x meters} \cr & \text{Then,} \frac{x+90}{12} = \frac{x+120}{15} \cr & \Rightarrow 15(x + 90) = 12(x + 120) \cr & \Leftrightarrow 15x + 1350 = 12x + 1440 \cr & \Leftrightarrow 3x = 90 \Leftrightarrow x = 30\cr & \text{Speed of the train} = \left(\frac{30+90}{12}\right)\cr & = 10 \space m/sec \cr & = \left(10 \times \frac{18}{5}\right) km/hr\cr & = 36 km/hr. \cr & \text{Hence, length of the train = 30 m,}\cr & \text{speed of the train = 36 km/hr}\cr } $$

4] Two trains 240 meters and 270 meters in length are running towards each other on parallel lined, one at the rate of 60 kmph and another at 48 kmph. How much time will they take to cross each other?
Answer: Option A
Solution:
$$ \eqalign{ & \text{Speed} = \left( 60 \times \frac{50}{3} \right)m/sec = \frac{50}{3}m/sec. \cr & \text{Relative speed of the two trains} \cr & \text{= (60 + 48) }kmph \cr & \text{= 108 kmph = } \left( 108 \times \frac{5}{18} \right)m/sec \cr & \text{= 30 } m/sec \cr & \text{Time taken by the trains to pass each} \cr & \text{other = Time taken to cover }\cr & \text{(240 + 270)}m \text{ at 30}m/sec = \left(\frac{510}{3}\right) \cr & =\text{17}sec \cr } $$

5] A 300 m long train passed a man walking along the line in the same direction at the rate of a 3 kmph in 33 seconds. Find the speed of the train in kmph(approx).
Answer: Option D
Solution:
$$ \eqalign{ &\text{Speed of the train relative to man}\\ &=\frac{300}{33}\space m/sec \space = \frac{100}{11}\space m/sec \\ &=\left( \frac{100}{11} \times \frac{18}{5}\right)\space km/hr = \frac{360}{11}\space kmph \\ &\text{Let the speed of the train be x km/hr.} \\ &\text{Then, relative speed = (x-3) kmph.} \\ &\therefore x-3 = \frac{360}{11} \\ &\Rightarrow x = \frac{360}{11} +3 = \frac{393}{11} = 35\frac{8}{11} \\ &\text{Hence, speed of the train} \\ &= 35 \frac{8}{11} \space kmph. }$$
